## four

/*fɔːr*/

> The number equivalent to the sum of one and one and one and one; a numeral representing this number.

**四**

- I have four apples in my basket.
- 我的篮子里有四个苹果。

*In some East Asian cultures, the number four is considered unlucky because its pronunciation is similar to the word for "death."*

## two

/*tuː*/

> Being one more than one.

**二**

- I have two cats.
- 我有两只猫。

*Avoid using "twos" as a plural noun. Use "pairs" or "couples" instead.*

## one

*/wʌn/*

> The number 1; a single thing or person.

**一**

- One apple is enough for me.
- 一个苹果对我来说就够了。

*Note that "one" can sometimes be used as a pronoun to avoid repeating a noun that was already mentioned. For example: "I need a blue pen. Do you have one?" Here, "one" refers to "a blue pen".*

## three

/*θriː/*

> The number equivalent to the sum of one and two; a symbol representing this number (3).

**三**

- I have three cats.
- 我有三只猫。

*Note that "three" can also be used as an adjective describing something that has three parts or elements.*
